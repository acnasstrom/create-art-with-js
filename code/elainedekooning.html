Recreating Elaine de Kooning's painting "Abstract"

[Elaine de Kooning's] paintings feature loose, thick brushstrokes in bold hues executed in an energetic, improvisational mode. “A painting to me is primarily a verb, not a noun,” she famously declared, “an event first and only secondarily an image.”
artnet.com

Abstract, 1970

This "Abstract" inspired painting is generated by drawing a fixed number of black lines and curves (broad paint "strokes") on a canvas.

The width, start and end point of the lines an curves are all generated by using random numbers fitting a Gaussian, or normal, distribution. There is theoretically no minimum or maximum value that randomGaussian() might return. Rather, there is just a very low probability that values far from the mean will be returned; and a higher probability that numbers near the mean will be returned.
